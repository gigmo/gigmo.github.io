I am a goldfish

I am a goldfish… I am searching for my identity. I am searching for a place where I belong. A place where my wounds can be cradled, my vibrant colours appreciated, and my sensitivity celebrated. I swim in circles waiting for someone to see me. My memory is shallow. My existence is not. Today, I mourn who I was yesterday. When tomorrow comes, I will yearn for who I am today. My memory is shallow. My existence is not. Truly, I mourn the individuality I lost in chasing faux notoriety. I mourn the child I abandoned in search for gold in the mines of capitalism. I mourn closeness. I mourn intellectualism for dual monologues only meant to pass idle time, villainize, and analyse until we can finally escape these prisons we institutionalize. Until when will we trade moral superiority for a chance to be happy. Truly. I am a goldfish… I forget. I do not forgive. Betrayal discomforts my innards like squid ink staining a patch of clear sea. Your younger self will save you.
